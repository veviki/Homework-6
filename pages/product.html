African yams

Yams are native to warmer regions of both hemispheres, and several species are cultivated us staple food croops in the tropics. In certain tropical cultures, notably in West Africa and new Guinea, the yamis the primary agricultural commodity and the focal point of elaborate. Yams are consumed as cooked starchy vegetables.

African Plantain

Plantains are major group of banana varieties(genus Musa) that are staple foods in many tropical areas. This edible fruit of plantain bananas has more starch than the common desert banana and is not eaten raw. Because plantains have the most starch before they ripen, they are usually cooked green, either boiled or fried, in sasvory dishes. The ripe fruits are mildly sweet and are often cooked with coconut juice or sugar as a flavouring. Plantains may also be dried for later use in cooking or ground for use as a meal, which can be further refined to a flour.

African white-garden-egg

Eggplants are a type of eggplant used as a food crop in several countries across Africa. These small, white fruits have a teardrop or roundish shape and are valued for their bitterness. Garden egg is indegenous to Sub-Saharan Africa and can be stored for up to three months by allowing it to dry, which is particularly usefu; in the tropical regions where refrigerators may be limited. In Ghana, garden eggs is one of the three most consumed vegetables, alongside tomatoes and peppers. It is used in stews and can also be eaten raw. Interestingly, the stems and leaves of garden eggs are consumed in some African countries, although not in Ghana. Due to its spongy texture, garden egg is sometimes used as a less expensive meat substitute, as it absorbs other lavours well.
